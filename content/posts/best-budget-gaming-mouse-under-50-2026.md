## 1. Logitech G203 Lightsync

- **Price:** ~$35
- **DPI:** 8,000
- **Weight:** 85g
- **Pros:** Great build quality, customizable RGB, reliable sensor
- **Cons:** Braided cable would be nice, side buttons could be bigger

The Logitech G203 is the gold standard for budget gaming mice. It has a solid sensor, durable construction, and Logitech's excellent G HUB software for customization.

## 2. Razer DeathAdder Essential

- **Price:** ~$30
- **DPI:** 6,400
- **Weight:** 96g
- **Pros:** Ergonomic shape, great for large hands, trusted brand
- **Cons:** No RGB, lower DPI than competitors

If you have larger hands or prefer palm grip, the DeathAdder Essential gives you Razer's iconic shape at a fraction of the cost.

## 3. SteelSeries Rival 3

- **Price:** ~$40
- **DPI:** 8,500
- **Weight:** 77g
- **Pros:** Lightweight, great sensor, excellent for competitive gaming

The Rival 3 punches above its weight class with a TrueMove Core optical sensor that delivers 1-to-1 tracking.

## 5. Corsair Katar Pro XT

- **Price:** ~$25
- **DPI:** 18,000
- **Weight:** 73g
- **Pros:** Ultra-lightweight, very affordable, high max DPI
- **Cons:** Basic build, no RGB

The Katar Pro XT is the best value pick. At just $25, you get an 18,000 DPI sensor and a lightweight design perfect for claw grip users.
